05 Update ‚Äî move the player & boxes
function update() {
  // Move player if key is held
  if (keys.left)  player.x -= player.speed;
  if (keys.right) player.x += player.speed;

  // Move every box down
  boxes.forEach(b => b.y += b.speed);

  // Remove boxes that left the screen
  boxes = boxes.filter(b => b.y < H);

  // Check if any box hit the player
  if (boxes.some(b => hits(b, player))) endGame();
}
06 Collision Detection ‚Äî did they touch?
function hits(a, b) {
  return (
    a.x < b.x + b.w &&
    a.x + a.w > b.x &&
    a.y < b.y + b.h &&
    a.y + a.h > b.y
  );
}

How to Play Dodge!

Dodge! is a minimalist arcade survival game with a single, unforgiving rule: don't let the falling boxes hit you. It's one of the purest reflex-testing games you'll find, offering an experience that anyone can pick up in seconds yet struggle to master for hours.

Your character is a small square at the bottom of the screen. You control it using the left and right arrow keys, or alternatively the A and D keys, to move horizontally. From above, an endless stream of boxes rains down. Your sole objective is to avoid being hit by any of them for as long as possible. Each second you survive adds to your score, and your longest survival time is recorded as your personal best.

The boxes fall at randomised positions and randomised speeds, so no two runs are ever quite the same. Early in a run the box density is manageable, but over time the spawn rate increases, filling the screen with more falling hazards simultaneously. The corridors you can safely navigate become narrower, and the decisions you need to make become faster.

Dodge! also doubles as an interactive coding tutorial. Switch to the Code tab to view the actual JavaScript source code that powers the game, broken down into annotated sections. Click any code block to receive a plain-English explanation of what it does. The Challenge tab then gives you a series of hands-on modifications to attempt, teaching you how to expand and customise the game yourself. Whether you're here to play or learn, Dodge! offers genuine value on both fronts.

Press Space or click the Play button to start a new game. When a box hits you, the game ends and your score is displayed. Press Space or click again to immediately restart ‚Äî the game is designed for rapid retry loops that keep your heart rate high and your fingers ready.

Tips & Tricks for Dodge!

Dodge! appears simple, but reaching truly high scores requires developing intuitions that separate casual players from skilled ones. These tips will accelerate your learning curve significantly.

  • Watch the top of the screen: The most common beginner mistake is focusing on your player square at the bottom rather than watching where boxes appear at the top. By the time a box is near the bottom of the screen, your reaction window is almost gone. Keep your eyes trained on the upper third of the screen to give yourself maximum time to move.
  • Stay near the centre: Hugging the edges limits your escape options to one direction. Staying near the horizontal centre of the screen allows you to dodge in either direction with equal freedom, making it far easier to respond to boxes spawning on either side.
  • Move in short bursts: Hold the direction key for just long enough to clear a gap, then release. Constant, deliberate tapping is more controlled than holding the key and sliding across the entire screen, which can overshoot gaps and carry you into a box on the other side.
  • Anticipate clusters: Sometimes multiple boxes spawn close together horizontally, forming a cluster with only small gaps between them. When you spot a tight cluster forming at the top of the screen, identify the largest gap in it early and start moving toward that gap before the cluster reaches the bottom.
  • Stay calm in dense phases: As the game escalates, the screen will fill with more boxes simultaneously. The temptation is to make frantic movements, but this usually causes you to run into a box you hadn't seen. Take a breath, slow your inputs, and make deliberate choices rather than reactive ones.
  • Use the edges strategically: While staying near the centre is generally best, the edges of the screen can occasionally be used as a temporary safe zone when the centre becomes blocked. A fast sprint to the edge followed by an immediate return to centre can thread a gap that would be impossible to navigate from a central position.
  • Learn from the code: The Code tab is more than just educational ‚Äî it shows you exactly how boxes are spawned and how their positions are randomised. Understanding the underlying logic helps you mentally model what patterns are possible, which improves your anticipation in-game.
  • Complete the challenges: The Challenge tab modifications aren't just coding exercises. Adding lives, speed scaling, or collectible stars changes the way you think about the game, giving you new respect for how small design changes can dramatically alter difficulty and fun. Completing challenges also deepens your intuition for how the core mechanics interact.

The highest-scoring Dodge! players share one trait: they are always looking ahead, never reacting to immediate threats but anticipating threats that haven't arrived yet. Train yourself to read the patterns forming at the top of the screen and you'll find your survival times climbing steadily with every session.
